Every founder hits the same wall: the team that shipped your MVP in weeks now takes months to deliver a single feature. You hire more engineers. It gets worse.

This is Brooks's Law in action — and it's been true for 50 years.

## The Real Problem

Adding people to a late project makes it later. But the deeper issue isn't headcount — it's **knowledge transfer overhead**. Every new hire needs context that only exists in the heads of your existing team.

## Patterns That Work

### 1. Document Decisions, Not Just Code

Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) are the single highest-ROI practice for growing teams. A new engineer reading "we chose PostgreSQL over MongoDB because..." is worth hours of onboarding meetings.

### 2. Automate the SDLC, Not Just Tests

CI/CD is table stakes. The real wins come from automating:

- Environment provisioning
- Code review checklists
- Deployment readiness checks
- Runbook generation

### 3. Build Knowledge Transfer Into Delivery

Don't treat onboarding as a separate activity. Pair programming, mob reviews, and rotating ownership create teams that scale without single points of failure.

## The TremTec Approach

That's the difference between scaling people and scaling capability.
